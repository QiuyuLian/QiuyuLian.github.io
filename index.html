About Me

I am an Assistant Research Professor at the University of Cambridge, working at the interface of artificial intelligence, mathematics, and biology.

My research focuses on developing AI-driven and mathematically principled models to understand how cell states evolve from high-dimensional molecular data.

I study these processes in the context of:

  • Stem cell fate decisions
  • Development
  • Tissue repair
  • Cancer
  • Ageing

Research

AI and Topological Modelling of Cell Fate Dynamics

My research aims to understand how cells make decisions over time, and how these processes are altered in disease.

  • Neural ODEs and dynamical systems for modelling trajectories
  • Topological data analysis (persistent homology, circular coordinates)
  • Single-cell and spatial omics data

Long-term Vision

To build a predictive and mechanistic framework of cell fate, enabling identification of key regulators, understanding disease progression, and discovery of therapeutic targets.
